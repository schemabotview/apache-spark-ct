Why are DataFrames faster than RDDs?

Tungsten runs your plan fast — but only DataFrames let it. Off-heap memory stores rows in binary buffers outside the JVM heap, skipping the garbage collector. Whole-stage codegen fuses a whole stage into one loop instead of one call per operator per row. An RDD has no schema, so Tungsten can't lay it out; a DataFrame's schema is a contract it compiles against.
