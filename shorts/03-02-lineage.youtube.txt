Spark doesn't back up your data

Unlike HDFS, Spark never replicates your RDD data. It remembers the recipe — the transformations that built each partition (the lineage graph). When an executor crashes, Spark replays just that slice of the lineage to rebuild the lost partition. Resilience = pure functions + lineage, not copies. (And it's why cache matters — without it, every action re-runs the full lineage.)
