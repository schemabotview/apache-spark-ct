Why does Spark always make 200 partitions?

A partition is Spark's unit of parallelism — one partition, one task, one core. More partitions than cores keeps cores busy; fewer than cores leaves them idle. After any shuffle, Spark defaults to spark.sql.shuffle.partitions = 200 — famous, and often wrong for your data. Right-sizing partitions is one of the biggest levers in Spark performance.
